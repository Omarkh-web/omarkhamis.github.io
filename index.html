About

Turning complexity
into clarity.

A career built on financial rigour, cross-cultural leadership, and the conviction that great audit work creates strategic advantage.

After 19 years at Ernst & Young — spanning Egypt, Kuwait, Qatar, Cairo, Oman, and now Dubai — one thing has stayed constant: the belief that a great audit isn't just about finding what's wrong. It's about building the confidence for organisations to grow right.

As Audit Director at EY Dubai, I lead large-scale engagements for listed and regulated entities across drilling, utilities, real estate, and government sectors. I partner with boards, audit committees, and executive leadership to embed risk-based thinking and strengthen governance frameworks.

I hold a CPA (USA) and CPIA certification, with deep expertise in IFRS, ISA, SOX, AML, and the COSO framework. I've led digital audit transformation initiatives and trained multinational teams across five countries.

The EY Advantage

What 19 years at the
world's top audit firm builds.

Big 4 experience isn't just a credential — it's a way of thinking, a global methodology, and a cross-sector lens that most finance professionals never develop.

When you've audited drilling giants in Oman, listed hospitality groups in Qatar, real estate developers in Cairo, and regulated multinationals in Dubai — all under EY's rigorous global methodology — you develop something money can't teach: pattern recognition at institutional scale.

Every engagement adds a new layer. You see how risk manifests differently in a power utility versus a manufacturing group. You understand why a listed company's governance framework fails in ways a private firm's wouldn't. You learn what a board actually needs to hear — and how to say it.

That breadth is the EY advantage. Not just technical depth in one area, but the ability to transfer frameworks, spot parallels, and solve problems that organisations have never seen before — because you've seen something like it somewhere else.

"The best auditors don't just verify what happened. They see what's coming — and help the organisation prepare."
